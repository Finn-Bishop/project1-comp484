Characters & Cast

The officers, crew, and souls aboard HMS Surprise

Principal Characters

At the center of the film is the friendship between two very different men united by mutual respect, shared music, and the close quarters of a wooden warship.

Captain Jack Aubrey

Portrayed by Russell Crowe

"Lucky Jack" Aubrey is the commanding officer of HMS Surprise, a natural leader whose boldness, tactical brilliance, and infectious confidence inspire fierce loyalty in his crew. He is a man of action first and foremost—decisive, courageous, and utterly committed to the Royal Navy and his duty to king and country.

Aubrey is also a lover of music, playing the violin in nightly duets with his friend Maturin. Beneath his jovial exterior lies a captain who understands the heavy cost of command and bears its weight with quiet resolve. His relentless pursuit of the Acheron reveals both his greatest strength—unyielding determination—and the way that same quality can become a dangerous obsession.

Dr. Stephen Maturin

Portrayed by Paul Bettany

The ship's surgeon and resident naturalist, Stephen Maturin is Aubrey's intellectual counterpart and closest companion. Where Aubrey is a man of instinct and martial courage, Maturin is a man of reason, science, and moral conviction.

A gifted physician and passionate student of the natural world, Maturin serves as the film's moral compass. His longing to explore the Galápagos Islands creates one of the story's central tensions, as Aubrey's obsession with the chase forces Maturin to repeatedly sacrifice his scientific ambitions. He plays the cello alongside Aubrey's violin, and their music together becomes a symbol of their bond.

Officers & Crew

The strength of the film lies in the depth of its ensemble. Every character aboard the Surprise feels vividly real, from seasoned officers to the youngest midshipmen.

1st Lt. Thomas Pullings

James D'Arcy

Aubrey's trusted first lieutenant and right-hand man. Pullings is steady, capable, and deeply loyal. He carries out his captain's orders with quiet efficiency and serves as a reassuring presence for both the officers and crew.

2nd Lt. William Mowett

Edward Woodall

The ship's second lieutenant, Mowett is an educated and sensitive officer with a talent for poetry. He provides a touch of gentility amidst the rough conditions of naval life and is a reliable leader in battle.

Preserved Killick

David Threlfall

Aubrey's long-suffering steward, Killick is cantankerous, fiercely protective of the captain's possessions, and endlessly grumbling. Despite his sharp tongue, his dedication to Aubrey is absolute. He provides much of the film's dry humor.

Midshipman Blakeney

Max Pirkis

A young midshipman of extraordinary courage and promise. After losing his arm in the opening battle, Blakeney refuses to be defined by his injury. He becomes a symbol of resilience, earning the admiration of the entire crew.

Midshipman Hollom

Lee Ingleby

A tragic figure, Hollom is an older midshipman who has never earned a promotion. Lacking the authority to command respect, he becomes the target of the crew's superstition that he is a "Jonah" bringing bad luck aboard the ship.

Barrett Bonden

Billy Boyd

The coxswain of the Surprise, Bonden is a skilled and dependable sailor who serves as Aubrey's personal helmsman. Calm under fire and respected by the crew, he is the kind of experienced seaman that holds a ship together.

Mr. Allen

Robert Pugh

The ship's master, responsible for navigation and the sailing of the vessel. Allen is an older, experienced seaman whose knowledge of winds and currents proves essential during the long pursuit around Cape Horn.

Midshipman Calamy

Max Benitz

One of the younger midshipmen, Calamy is bright, eager, and quick-witted. He represents the next generation of naval officers and shows great promise throughout the voyage.
